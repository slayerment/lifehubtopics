What‚Äôs broadly agreed on

  • On average, finishing college is associated with higher earnings and lower unemployment.
  • Differences between majors and fields are large ‚Äì not all degrees have similar ROI.
  • Completion matters more than ‚Äústarting‚Äù ‚Äì many costs are sunk if you don‚Äôt finish.
  • Debt can be manageable or crushing depending on amount, field, and earnings.

Where experts still debate

  • The value of degrees vs alternative credentials in fast-moving fields (tech, design, etc.).
  • How to design college so first-gen and working students actually graduate.
  • Long-term impact of high student debt on life choices (kids, home, career risk-taking).

FAQ & related topics
Questions people actually ask when deciding what to go to college for.
Is college even worth it anymore? ‚ñ∏
It depends on your LifeMap. On average, degrees still correlate with higher earnings and lower unemployment, but there‚Äôs huge variation by field, cost, and whether you finish. Some people do much better with cheaper or more targeted paths. This topic helps you see where you likely fall.
What if I pick the wrong major? ‚ñ∏
Many people change majors. The bigger question is whether your early choices keep options open and whether you‚Äôre learning transferable skills. A good plan makes it possible to pivot without throwing everything away.
Should I go to community college first? ‚ñ∏
For many LifeMaps, starting at a community college can cut costs and buy time to explore. For some high-aid or highly targeted programs, going straight to a four-year or specialized program makes more sense. The right answer depends on your money, grades, and clarity.
