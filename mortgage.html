Should I skip other bills to pay the mortgage? ‚ñ∏
It depends. Shelter is usually close to the top of the list, but so are food, utilities, and transport. High-interest credit cards may feel urgent, but missing housing payments can have bigger consequences. A real plan should weigh interest rates, late fees, credit impact, and what‚Äôs safest for your situation.

Not necessarily. For some LifeMaps, exiting or downsizing early can reduce stress, protect credit, and free up money for other goals. For others, staying put with a restructure makes more sense. The key is making that choice before you‚Äôre forced into it.

Simply calling usually doesn‚Äôt hurt your score. Missed payments, collections, and foreclosure do. Some options may still impact your credit, but often less than doing nothing. Details depend on your loan, your lender, and the programs available.
