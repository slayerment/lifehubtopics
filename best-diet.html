Evidence & what‚Äôs debated
Diet research is messy, but some patterns are surprisingly consistent.
Nerd lane available

What‚Äôs broadly agreed on

  • Diets rich in minimally processed plants, with adequate protein, are generally linked with better long-term health.
  • Ultra-processed foods and sugar-sweetened beverages are consistently associated with worse outcomes.
  • Weight, body composition, and metabolic markers are influenced by overall intake and quality, not a single nutrient.
  • Sustainability and adherence matter at least as much as specific diet branding.

Where experts still debate

  • Ideal carb vs fat split, especially for people with insulin resistance.
  • How much red / processed meat is compatible with long-term health.
  • The role of ultra-low carb or ketogenic diets beyond specific medical indications.
  • How to interpret observational data with strong cultural and lifestyle confounders.

FAQ & related topics
Questions people actually ask when trying to find a sustainable diet.
Do calories matter, or is it all hormones? ‚ñ∏
Both energy balance and hormones matter, and they affect each other. For many people, focusing on food quality, protein, fiber, sleep, and stress indirectly improves hormones and makes it easier to naturally eat at a level that fits their goals.
Should I cut out carbs completely? ‚ñ∏
Some people benefit from lowering certain types of carbs, especially refined starches and sugar, but that doesn‚Äôt always mean zero-carb. Your LifeMap (and clinician, if you have conditions) matters here. Cutting carbs aggressively without a plan can backfire for some people.
Is keto / carnivore / vegan ‚Äúthe best‚Äù? ‚ñ∏
Each of those patterns can help some people and be problematic for others. What matters is how they intersect with your health status, labs, mental health, environment, and preferences. This topic helps you see levers and evidence instead of choosing a team by identity.
What if I have (or had) an eating disorder? ‚ñ∏
If you have a history of disordered eating, any diet change is best done with support from qualified professionals who understand that history. Some LifeHub modules and journeys may be inappropriate or need modifying in that context.
