FAQ & related topics
Questions people actually ask when trying to help without burning out.
How do I know if I‚Äôm actually helping? ‚ñ∏
You can‚Äôt control everything, but you can look for signals: what people say, what changes, how organizations use your time or money, and whether your actions line up with what they say they need. Over time, checking in and adjusting is more useful than obsessing over a perfect metric.
How do I help without burning out? ‚ñ∏
Burnout usually comes from mismatched expectations and invisible limits. Getting honest about your capacity, setting boundaries, and choosing a few clear lanes matters more than trying to respond to every request or crisis you see online.

Not if ‚Äúfirst‚Äù is about building a stable platform from which you can help for a long time. If your health, finances, or safety are collapsing, stabilizing yourself is often the most responsible step. This topic helps you see when that‚Äôs the case vs when you‚Äôre just avoiding discomfort.
